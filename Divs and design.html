Formula 1 Racing

Very Brief Intro to Formula 1


  • Formula 1 more commonly known as F1 refers to to highest level of international racing
  • F1 championships occur yearly and they consist of 10 constructors(which are teams) with 2 drivers per constructor
  • The races are known as Grands Prix
  • Grands Prix occur all over the world and each year there is a diffrent number of grands prix depending on the countries taking place in the championship
  • At the end of the season there are 2 awards: Constructors' Award and Drivers' Award
  • These awards are given based on the points they gain during the season
  • The point-system works as follows:
    • In each grand prix points are given to the drivers that place in the top 10
      1. Winner takes 25 points
      2. 2nd place takes 18 points
      3. 3rd place takes 15 points
      4. While numbers 4-10 take 12, 10, 8, 6, 4, 2 and 1
    • The points that each driver gets in the grands prix are added togther
    • While for each constructor, the points of both it's drivers are added together
    • At the end of the season, the constructor with most overall points wins the Constructors' Award while the driver with the most points wins the Drivers' Award
